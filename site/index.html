What it actually is

Two explanations, pick yours

If you've never traded

Imagine a machine that reads every price on India's largest stock exchange each evening, picks 25 companies to hold, places the orders, checks its own books, and messages you if something looks wrong.

Now the hard part: proving the machine is skilled rather than lucky. Financial data will happily hand you a beautiful answer that is completely false. Most of this project is the proving.

If you trade

Point-in-time NSE panel from primary bhavcopy plus the declared corporate-action feed (delistings included, factors verified against ex-day prices both ways). Weekly cross-sectional momentum through Ledoit-Wolf min-variance construction with Gârleanu-Pedersen partial adjustment, vol targeting, ADV caps, T+1-close execution, full Indian cost stack.

Validation: purged walk-forward + CPCV, deflated Sharpe against an append-only trial ledger, White Reality Check and Hansen SPA. Backtest/engine parity is a CI gate.

Read that table honestly. The live book earns less raw return than the index (13.7% vs 14.97%) at lower volatility — which is why its Sharpe is higher. It's a risk-adjusted win, not a return win. After Indian short-term capital-gains tax the CAGR is 11.0%.

Most projects report the best figure they found. This one reports the figure that survives being counted.

The headline finding

A popular class of forecasting papers is measuring look-ahead

A large applied literature reports spectacular daily equity forecasting after decomposition preprocessing — EMD, CEEMDAN, wavelet hybrids. Information coefficients above 0.3, Sharpe ratios above 3.

We reproduced those numbers exactly on ICICI Bank. Then we changed one thing: instead of decomposing the whole price history at once, we recomputed the identical transform causally — at each day using only data available up to that day, which is the only version you could ever actually trade.

The entire edge vanished. That's what a global transform applied before a temporal split does to you — every "training" sample silently becomes a function of future prices. Written up as a working paper in the repo.

The real contribution

Seven things that don't work

Nulls are the expensive, useful output of research — and the ones nobody publishes.

  1. Machine learning doesn't beat momentum. Ridge, LightGBM, MLP and a transformer under one purged protocol. PBO 0.86 — the in-sample winner is overfit 24 times out of 28 splits.
  2. Post-earnings drift runs backwards in India. 1.48M timestamped exchange announcements; the biggest positive surprises reverse (t = −6.9).
  3. Momentum-crash regime gates add nothing beyond the volatility targeting already shipped.
  4. The single-name model zoo loses to buy-and-hold. GRU, LSTM, transformer, ensemble — all under half the always-long floor after costs. Retraining cadence turned out irrelevant: the problem is absence of signal, not staleness.
  5. News sentiment gating subtracts value (0.06 vs 0.58 Sharpe) — consistent with the inverted drift above.
  6. EWMA covariance doesn't beat Ledoit-Wolf — faster adaptation, paid for in churn.
  7. A learned trading-speed policy ties the fixed constant. A contextual bandit over 728 weekly decisions: PBO 0.93, near-uniform action counts — the agent itself reporting that the objective surface is flat and the analytical constant is already near-optimal.
Credibility

Four times this project was wrong — in public

A research system's credibility isn't how good its numbers look. It's what happens when they're wrong.

The data feed lied

The exchange's declared corporate-action feed contained a 1:5 stock split that never happened, manufacturing a +398% phantom return. The adjuster now verifies every declared factor against the ex-day price and rejects contradictions — catching 14 phantom events across sixteen years.

We caught ourselves inflating a headline

A construction upgrade first measured Sharpe 1.119 with a −21% drawdown. Our own code review found a position-cap bug quietly parking money in cash — accidental de-risking. Corrected to 1.018. Both numbers stay in the record.

We corrected our own significance claim

"The strategy family beats the index (p = 0.0415)" turned out to be carried by a naive baseline inside that family. Across constructed configurations alone, p = 0.655. The claim is now stated precisely: a risk-adjusted win, not a raw-return win.

A promising upgrade was refused

A blended signal scored Sharpe 1.30 against the live 1.02 — then failed two of four pre-registered gates. It was held, not shipped. The friendlier re-test that might have rescued it was deliberately never run.

Engineering

It runs itself, and watches itself

01

Point-in-time data

Immutable raw zone with sha256 manifests. Every delisted company retained — survivorship bias measured at +2.5pp/yr of imaginary alpha.

02

Proving it isn't luck

Purged CV, CPCV, PBO, deflated Sharpe, Reality Check/SPA — and a ledger that counts every experiment ever run, forever.

03

Trading it

Backtest/engine parity enforced in CI. Deterministic order ids make a crashed rerun unable to double-trade. Drawdown rails halve or freeze the book automatically.

04

Watching itself

Signal-decay and feature-drift alarms daily. A heartbeat that fires on silence — the failure that otherwise looks exactly like success.

An alert nobody receives isn't an alert. Every alarm is written durably to disk before any push channel is attempted. Nothing auto-remediates — alarms inform, humans decide.

There's also a research agent that proposes new features, executes them inside an AST-whitelisted sandbox (model output is never run unaudited), screens them under the standard protocol, and keeps a posterior over which kinds of idea have historically paid off. It has demonstrably changed its mind: after two negative liquidity screens, it reordered what it tries next. Knowledge compounds; risk doesn't, because it never touches the live book.

Artha — built by VJ. Python, polars, uv, mypy --strict, 243 tests including a look-ahead suite and a backtest/live parity gate.

Limitations are disclosed, not hidden: synthetic TRI benchmark, static sector map, cash at 0%, paper slippage degenerate until live quotes, DSR 0.20 counted conservatively. A limitation you disclose can't ambush you later.
